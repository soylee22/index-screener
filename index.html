The thesis: buy quality on a discount. The Quality score (0-100) ranks each name against its industry peers (banks vs banks, payment networks vs networks, insurers vs insurers) on profitability (ROE, margins), balance-sheet strength (low leverage, free cash flow) and growth, plus a bonus for a sustainable dividend (shown as the green yield). Industry-relative on purpose, so a bank is not ranked against Visa and an asset-light name does not win automatically; beta is excluded because low-volatility is not quality. Hover a score for the underlying numbers. ★ Buy stars = the volatility-adjusted Dip-Buy Meter from the 3-year high (1/2/3 stars at ~1 / 1.5 / 2 vol-units of pullback), an amber ● = within 0.25 of the next tier. The buy list is the overlap: high Quality and stars, use the ◆ High quality + ★ Has buy signal toggles together. % from ATH tags the peak year (old year = structural decliner, recent = compounder near its high; NEW = short history, IPO caution). Minervini/Stage-2 is kept as an optional trend column.

Why 3-year, not all-time? On a chart you want all-time (it never heals artificially). On a screener snapshot you want rolling: it measures the current pullback and a NatWest-type that crashed in 2008 and never recovered correctly shows no signal (it is not down from its recent high), even though its all-time drawdown is −90%. Different tools, different reference. Stars alone are not a buy, pair them with a healthy Minervini score and a recent ATH year, then check the chart.
