How to read it. ★ Buy stars come from the volatility-adjusted Dip-Buy Meter: 1 star = pulled back ~1 vol-unit below its all-time high, 2 stars ~1.5, 3 stars ~2.0, i.e. an unusually deep dip for that stock's own volatility. Minervini shows how many of the 8 trend-template criteria the stock passes; the green STAGE 2 badge means a confirmed uptrend. The sweet spot is a name with both: a confirmed Stage-2 uptrend and stars (it has dipped), use the ★ Has buy signal + ▲ Stage 2 only toggles together to isolate them. Default sort is by market cap (biggest first); click any column to re-sort, or sort by ★ Buy to put the deepest signals on top.

Honesty. Stars alone do not mean buy. A deep dip with a low Minervini score is a falling knife (down a lot and still falling), not a bargain, and a single stock's drawdown can be permanent (it is not an index that must recover). Drawdown is from the all-time high in the local data; a few LSE names with messy split history may show an inflated drawdown. Use this as a research filter, then check the chart.
